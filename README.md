# Wikipedia2Markdown

Download articles from Wikipedia. And format them into Markdown.

**Example output:**

```
# 3DO Interactive Multiplayer

**3DO** is a [video gaming](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Video_gaming) hardware format developed by [The 3DO Company](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_3DO_Company) and conceived by [Electronic Arts](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Electronic_Arts) founder [Trip Hawkins](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trip_Hawkins). The specifications were originally designed by [Dave Needle](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dave_Needle) and [RJ Mical](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/RJ_Mical) of New Technology Group, and were licensed by third parties; most hardware were packaged as [home video game consoles](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Home_video_game_console) under the name ***Interactive Multiplayer***, and [Panasonic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Panasonic) produced the first models in 1993 with further renditions released afterwards by manufacturers [GoldStar](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/GoldStar), [Sanyo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sanyo), [Creative Labs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Creative_Labs), and [Samsung Electronics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Samsung_Electronics).

Centered around a 32-bit [ARM60](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_ARM_processors) processor and a custom graphics chip, the format was initially marketed as a [multimedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Multimedia) one but this shifted into purely video games within a year of launching. a year after offloading its [M2](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/3DO_M2) successor hardware to Panasonic.
```
